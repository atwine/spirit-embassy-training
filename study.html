Chapter 6 · Cardinal Moses

The Realm of Faith vs. The Realm of Senses

There is a question every believer must answer before they can walk in consistent victory:

Do you act after you see results? Do you act after you feel better? Or do you act when circumstances change?

The answer the Ra'ah gives is none of these. You act now. Faith keeps you in the now. This chapter shows you why — and what it costs you if you stay in the wrong realm.

The Two Realms

There are two realms every believer must understand and choose between.

The Realm of Senses is everything you can see, smell, taste, touch, and feel. It is the physical world — the natural realm. It is what your body reports to you, what your bank account shows you, what your circumstances project at you.

The Realm of Faith is the spiritual world. It is the Word of God — the word of promise, things declared but not yet manifested in the physical. The realm of faith is not governed by what you see. It is governed by what God has said.

These two realms are in constant tension, and you are always living in one of them.

The Stakes of the Realm You Choose

"If you keep Satan in the realm of faith, you will destroy his plans. But if he keeps you in the realm of sense, he will kill you." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

This is not a small distinction. It is the difference between victory and defeat. When you live in the realm of senses, everything around you points to defeat. Your symptoms speak. Your bank balance speaks. Your circumstances speak. And if you listen, fear takes over — because everything in the natural realm tells you that you are losing.

But when you live in the realm of faith, you have a vantage point. You see beyond what the natural shows. From that position, you destroy every plan of the enemy.

The choice of realm is yours to make.

Faith Does Not Make Sense

One of the most important things to settle in your mind is this: faith does not make sense.

You cannot explain faith with logic. You cannot put it on a grade scale. You cannot prove it with a scientific formula. This is because faith does not belong to the realm of senses — it belongs to the realm of the Spirit. And the realm of the Spirit does not make sense to the natural mind.

When the Word of God says "I am rich" and your bank account is in the red, that contradicts what you see in the physical. That is exactly the point. That is the realm of faith the Ra'ah is talking about.

Do not try to make faith logical. It was never meant to be.

Faith Is Always Present Tense

"Now faith is the substance of things hoped for, the evidence of things not seen." — Hebrews 11:1 (KJV)

The Ra'ah draws attention to the very first word: now.

Not "faith was." Not "faith will be." Now faith is.

This is not by coincidence. God uses words intentionally — He named Himself after the Word, and He will not waste it. The word now is there because faith is always a present-tense reality. Faith lives in the timeline of God, not in the timeline of chronology. And God lives in the eternal now — He has no yesterday, He has no tomorrow. He only has now.

This means that to say "God is going to heal me" is not a statement of God-kind faith. You are speaking in the future tense. You are putting God in tomorrow. But God is not in tomorrow — He is in now. When you speak faith, you speak in the now.

Faith is always present tense.

Faith vs. Hope — An Important Distinction

There is a difference between faith and hope that most believers miss.

Hope says: "God is going to heal me. God will provide for me."

Faith says: "I am healed. My needs are met. It is done."

When you say "God is going to give me something," you are not speaking a statement of God-kind faith. You are still placing the provision in the future. But God has already provided. The question is not whether He will — it is whether you will receive what He has already done.

Stop saying God will heal you. Start saying: I am healed.

Stop saying God will provide. Start saying: My needs are met.

This is the language of faith. This is the language of the realm of the Spirit.

It Is Already Done

"Who his own self bare our sins in his own body on the tree, that we, being dead to sins, should live unto righteousness: by whose stripes ye were healed." — 1 Peter 2:24 (KJV)

Notice the tense: ye were healed. Past tense. It happened 2,000 years ago.

The Ra'ah is clear: you do not need God to heal you again. He already healed you. What you need to do is receive what He has already provided. Walk into it. Claim it. Declare it.

When it comes to healing, it is a done deal. You are not looking into the future for something that has not happened yet. You are reaching back to what was already accomplished and saying: this is mine.

Facts vs. Truth

This is one of the most critical distinctions in the Ra'ah's teaching.

Facts are what your senses tell you. Facts change. Facts are temporary. They belong to the physical realm.

Truth is what God's Word says. Truth never changes. Truth is eternal. It is permanent.

These are not the same thing — and they are often in direct contradiction.

The Ra'ah is very precise here: we do not deny the facts. You do not have to pretend that pain does not exist. You do not have to lie and say the symptom is not there. That would be dishonest. But you do not have to agree with the facts. You do not have to give them authority. You do not have to let them control your confession.

You can say: "Yes, I feel pain — but the truth says I am healed. I agree with the truth."

"Sense is not the standard. Truth is the standard." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

Your feelings are not the standard. Your symptoms are not the standard. Your bank account is not the standard. God's Word is the standard. And if God's Word contradicts everything you see, God's Word is still right.

The Ra'ah's Testimony — His Son and the Kidney Scan

The Ra'ah shares this testimony consistently because it is one of the most powerful demonstrations of facts versus truth.

When one of his sons was born, the doctors ran tests and told the Ra'ah that the boy had only one kidney. Every scan confirmed it. The medical report was clear — one kidney.

But the Ra'ah refused to agree.

He kept saying: "I see two kidneys."

Not because he was denying the scan. Not because he was lying. But because he was choosing to agree with what God's Word said over what the machine showed. He understood that the scan was showing a fact — but facts are temporary. The truth was that his son was healed, because healing had already been accomplished 2,000 years ago.

The doctors were frustrated. They thought perhaps he did not understand the results. But the Ra'ah understood perfectly. He was operating in the realm of faith.

Weeks later, at the next checkup, the boy was found to have two perfectly functioning kidneys.

This is what it looks like to choose truth over facts. Truth always wins — because truth is God's Word, and God's Word does not fail.

The Radio Wave Illustration

The Ra'ah gives a powerful illustration to explain how the spiritual realm works.

Right now, the air around you is full of FM and AM radio waves. You cannot see them. You cannot touch them. You cannot smell them. But they are there — carrying voices, music, conversations — all existing in the invisible realm around you.

If you have a radio with the right receiver and you tune it to the right frequency, suddenly you can hear everything that was already there. The waves did not arrive when you turned on the radio. They were already present. The radio simply gave you access to what already existed.

"Faith is your receiver. The Bible is your frequency guide." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

When you tune in with faith, you pick up what is already there in the realm of the Spirit. Your healing is already there. Your provision is already there. Your breakthrough is already there — waiting to be received.

Every Material First Existed in the Spirit Realm

"Every material was first spiritual before God made it into the physical. The origin of every material is spiritual." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

Your house existed in the spirit realm before it existed on earth. Your healing existed in the spirit realm before it manifested in your body. Your money existed in the spirit realm before it appeared in your bank account.

This means that the solution to every problem you are facing is not somewhere out there in the future. It already exists — in the realm of the Spirit. What you are doing through faith is reaching into that realm and pulling the manifestation down into the physical.

Everything you need is already there. Faith is how you receive it.

  • Never say "God is going to bless me" — say: I am blessed. I am increased.
  • Keep a journal. Every time you catch yourself using future tense, write it down.
  • At the end of 7 days, review your journal and share your progress.

God lives in the eternal now. Your faith must operate in His timeline.

Chapter 7 · The Seer

How Faith Grows — The God-Kind Faith Accelerator

This is the God-Kind Faith Accelerator Course — which means your life is going to be accelerated supernaturally.

But before that can happen, something must be addressed.

The Spiritual Bankruptcy Among Believers

"It is evident through the language and common use of terminology that there is a spiritual bankruptcy within a lot of believers. There is a serious deficit — and it is common through the use of terminology and wording." — The Seer

The evidence? How many believers have prayed this prayer: "Lord, increase my faith."

Most Christians have prayed it. Some have even fasted for God to increase their faith. And yet — this chapter will show you from Scripture why this prayer, as common as it is, is not what the Word of God teaches. And more importantly, exactly how faith actually grows.

You Cannot Pray for More Faith — It Is Unscriptural

"And the apostles said unto the Lord, Increase our faith. And the Lord said, If ye had faith as a grain of mustard seed, ye might say unto this sycamine tree, Be thou plucked up by the root, and be thou planted in the sea; and it should obey you." — Luke 17:5-6 (KJV)

Notice carefully what Jesus did — and what He did not do.

He never laid His hands on them. He never prayed for them. He never said: "I will ask the Father to give you more faith." Instead, He gave them a parable about a servant who went straight to work.

"Increasing faith is a commandment, not a prayer request." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

Because it is a commandment, it cannot be prayed for. Jesus was not saying "I'll help you get more faith." He was saying: "I already told you what to do — now do it." Study the Word. Act on the Word. Obey the Word. Faith will come automatically as a result of following those principles.

There is no supernatural download of faith available through prayer. There are no steps in the realm of the Spirit that can be skipped.

No Steps Can Be Skipped — No Microwave Christianity

"Although I am the blood and son of the man of God, the lessons I had to go through were really intense — because in the school of the Spirit, no lessons can be skipped." — The Seer

Many people want microwave Christianity. They want to sit under the man of God, receive a prophecy, get a hand laid on them, and come out the other side with great faith. But that is not how the Kingdom works.

You cannot wish your way into more faith. You cannot pray your way into more faith. There is only one way.

Faith Carries an Expression — It Can Be Seen

Faith is not invisible. It carries a countenance. It can be seen.

"Faith can be seen upon your countenance. We can see faith littered in your language. We can see faith in your terminology. We can see your faith." — The Seer

The Lord Jesus said to the centurion: "I have not seen great faith like this." He saw it. Faith expresses itself the same way love expresses itself — through action.

A man cannot tell his wife "I love you" if there are no groceries in the house when he has the means to provide them. She will question that love — because love has an expression. So does faith.

Faith is a doing word. It is a verb.

Romans 10:17 — Three Pillars, Not Two

"So then faith cometh by hearing, and hearing by the word of God." — Romans 10:17 (KJV)

This is one of the most misquoted verses in the Bible. Most people summarize it as: "Faith comes by the Word of God." But that is not what the verse says. Read it carefully.

The Ra'ah teaches that there are three distinct pillars in this verse:

  1. The Word of God
  2. Hearing
  3. Faith

And they operate in a specific sequence.

First — The Word of God produces the faculty of hearing. The Word of God does not go directly to faith. It first produces something in you — a faculty of hearing. Even a person who is physically deaf can hear by the Spirit of God, because faith, as they receive the Word, creates a hearing faculty in them through their spirit.

Second — When you hear the Word of God, faith comes.

Notice: faith does not come by the Word itself. Faith comes by hearing the Word. These are not the same thing. And this distinction is everything.

Akouo — Hearing That Acts

"Hearing, in the biblical sense, is not just listening. Hearing is acting on what you hear." — The Seer

The Greek word used in Romans 10:17 is akouo — and it means to understand and to obey. It means to get to a point of understanding, and then to get to a point of obedience. It is not passive. It is not merely receptive. It carries action within the very definition of the word.

Many believers have understood without obeying. They have listened without actually hearing. They have sat in services, received good teaching, nodded along, and walked away unchanged. In the biblical sense — they did not hear.

"Hearing the Word of God and acting upon it is how you effectively increase faith." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

You can listen to a sermon and walk away unchanged. That is not hearing. You can read your Bible and forget what you read by afternoon. That is not hearing.

Real hearing produces obedience. Obedience produces increased faith.

"But be ye doers of the word, and not hearers only, deceiving your own selves." — James 1:22 (KJV)

If you only listen, the Bible says you have been deceived. You think you have heard, but you have not.

The Lame Man at Lystria — Action Precedes Manifestation

"And there sat a certain man at Lystra, impotent in his feet, being a cripple from his mother's womb, who never had walked: The same heard Paul speak: who stedfastly beholding him, and perceiving that he had faith to be healed, Said with a loud voice, Stand upright on thy feet. And he leaped and walked." — Acts 14:8-10 (KJV)

The Bible says: "The same heard Paul speak." The Greek word for heard here is akouo — he understood, and he obeyed.

Paul did not touch this man. Paul perceived that he had faith to be healed. How? Because the man's actions showed it. He was listening. He was leaning forward. He was ready to obey. His countenance communicated faith.

Then Paul commanded: "Stand upright on thy feet." And the man leaped and walked.

The progression is deliberate:

  1. The man heard the Word (akouo — understood and prepared to obey)
  2. He acted on what he had heard
  3. The healing manifested

The action came before the manifestation. He did not wait to feel healing in his legs before he stood. He stood up — and then the healing came.

"Action precedes manifestation. You do not wait for the result. You act, and results follow." — The Seer

Faith Is Like a Muscle

Faith must be exercised. No one can lift weights for you.

You cannot believe beyond your current measure of faith — and attempting to do so does not produce results, it produces frustration and discouragement.

"Do not try and attempt to believe beyond your own measurement of faith." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

Start where you are. Start small. Act on the Word at your current level. See results. Then believe for bigger things. Then more. Then greater. Over time, your faith becomes strong.

  • —If you have never seen a miracle, do not start by believing for resurrection. Start with a headache.
  • —If you have never ridden a bike, do not start by believing for a jet. Start by believing for a bus pass.
  • —If you have never experienced financial breakthrough, do not start by believing for millions. Start with a specific small need. See it met. Then grow.

Do Not Treat Faith Like a 9-1-1 Call

"Many Christians treat faith like a 9-1-1 prayer for a spare tyre — utilizing God as an errand boy when things only go wrong." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

They never think of using faith until they desperately need it. And then, when they put it to work, they wonder why it is not performing — because it has not been exercised all along.

Do not wait for crisis. Exercise your faith daily on small things. Build your faith muscles. Then when the storm comes, your faith will be strong enough to handle it.

The Mustard Seed — Capacity, Not Smallness

Many believers have heard the mustard seed passage and concluded: "All you need is a little faith."

But look at how Jesus speaks about little faith throughout the Gospels:

"Why are ye fearful, O ye of little faith?" — Matthew 8:26 (KJV)
"O thou of little faith, wherefore didst thou doubt?" — Matthew 14:31 (KJV)
"Shall he not much more clothe you, O ye of little faith?" — Matthew 6:30 (KJV)

This is not a compliment. There is nothing commendable about little faith in Scripture. The Lord is not applauding little faith — He is rebuking it.

So what was Jesus saying with the mustard seed?

"The mustard seed is not about small faith. It is about the seed's ability to grow into a huge tree." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

A mustard seed is tiny — but it grows into a plant 10 to 15 feet tall. When Jesus said "have faith as a grain of mustard seed," He was talking about capacity — the God-given ability within you to grow.

The issue is not the size of your faith. The issue is whether your faith is growing. Little faith is a problem. Great faith is the goal. And faith becomes great when being exercised, being fed, and being acted upon.

A Personal Lesson from the Ra'ah — No Rainy Days

The Seer shares something the Ra'ah taught him personally.

The Seer once told his father that he had money saved up "for a rainy day." The Ra'ah's response:

"I used to also save money for a rainy day — until I got to a point where I decreed and declared in my life: there shall be no rainy day." — The Ra'ah, Prophet Uebert Angel

You do not prepare for defeat. You decree and declare the provision of God over your life — in the present tense, in the now — and you live by that declaration.
